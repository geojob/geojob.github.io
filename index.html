Mechanical Wheelchair

Designed and tested a full Mechanical standing wheelchair that utilizes linear springs to achieve three different standing angles. The stress/strain analysis was completed on Ansys and the chair was modeled on Solidoworks/CATIA. Using the dials on the double acting cylinders, the speed of the overall system transition was controlled. The plunger was attached to the armrests on the torso frame of the wheelchair to allow the user to move from a standing to a seated position at will.

Evolutionary Algorithm trained walking bot

An 8 vertex bot was trained using an evolutionary/genetic algorithm to essentially "walk" as far as possible. Vertices were added to the bot based on a random coefficient to increase diversity within the population. A population of random individuals was created and the best walking iterations were reproduced with one another and mutated based on the mutation coefficient. This was achieved by learning the spring coefficients between mass points (vertices). The mutation allowed the bot to travel towards the peak whereas the crossover within the reproduction method allowed to jump peaks to find the global maximum.

English Premier League Predictor

A machine learning model was created to predict the EPL League results for the 2015/2106 season. Data from the league as well as the champions league was collected from 2000 till 2014. A random forest classified and SVM were used along with Xgboost and a MLP classifier to learn the data which consisted of home/away records as well as wins,losses,goals scores and goals conceded.

Hyperloop Controls and Design

This was part of the SpaceX Hyperloop competition that took place in 2019. I worked on the design of the shell was first modelled in Solidoworks and verified for its aerodynamic properties on Ansys before finalizing the part. Additionally, I designed the brake pad in a similar way. However, after analyzing the part on Altair, a lattice optimization was applied to the part to reduce the overall mass of the brake system. I was also part of the controls team mainly putting together the CANbus framework along with the IMU and accelerometers to enable communication between the brakepads and the propulsion system.

RNN for Classical music

A recurrent neural network was built to generate classical music. The input consisted of various MIDI files from composers like Bach, Beethoven, Mozart and Deubussy. The data was converted to a state matrix and fed into the model consisting of custom LSTM layers. The model was trained to reduce the loss heuristic which was derived from the maximum likelihood estimate comparing the output and the input.

Robot Manipulation

ROS was used in conjuction with Python and C++ to create an environment to test Cartesian Control and inverse kinematics on a robot arm. The 7 dof Kuka LWR arm was used to test the algorithms. In addition to the basic programs, a rapidly exploring random tree algorithm was used with various ostacles to ensure the robot arm is able to move the end-effector to the desired location. Other robotics projects include employing an extended kalman filter to record the state of a moving 2 dimensional robot as well as implementing a dynamics engine with an aritrary number of links using a Newton-Euler algorithm.
